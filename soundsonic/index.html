Signal Noise

Sound Art

Watch on YouTube

Signal Noise frames media as a form of environmental noise. It begins in a natural setting, with wind, distant birds, and footsteps creating a sense of calm and presence. That clarity is quickly interrupted by a digital ringtone, marking the shift from the physical world into constant connection.

After the call is picked up, the soundscape becomes dense and disorienting. Filtered voices, mechanical hums, ticking clocks, and distorted everyday sounds begin to layer and overlap. They lose distinction, reflecting how information, communication, and obligations merge into a single overwhelming experience. A heartbeat runs through the piece, gradually accelerating before collapsing, grounding the sound in a physical response.

All sounds are recorded from real-life sources, including footsteps, wrappers, breath, glass, and ambient noise. These are then processed and manipulated, showing how digital systems reshape ordinary experiences into something more intense and unnatural.

Toward the end, the layers begin to fall away. The breathing fades, wind returns, and birds slowly reappear. This moment suggests a brief sense of clarity before the cycle can begin again.
