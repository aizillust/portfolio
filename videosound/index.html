Resonance

Video & Sound Art

Watch on YouTube

This piece explores how digital overstimulation affects memory and attention through image and sound.

Resonance builds on Signal Noise by pairing its soundscape with a visual narrative. It begins with a simple walk across campus, placing the viewer in a familiar and grounded setting. When the ringtone cuts the scene to black, the video shifts into a more internal space.

Breathing becomes the main visual rhythm. Each inhale and exhale moves between different locations, such as the sky, the Legion of Honor, and the art building. These transitions reflect moments of clarity, while the shifts between them suggest drifting attention.

As the heartbeat enters, the visuals change. Still images from my walk begin to appear, advancing with each beat. What starts as a normal routine becomes fragmented, turning into a sequence of memory rather than continuous experience. As the heartbeat speeds up, the images move faster and begin to mix with flashes of daily life and digital interactions, showing how everything blends together at peak overstimulation.

The video eventually slows and returns to the opening scene, forming a loop. This repetition reflects how attention moves between focus and distraction, never fully settling.
