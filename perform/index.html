The Offering

Video Performance-Theme:Actions

Watch on YouTube

The Offering shifts focus from the screen to the person serving it. While Sine Fine examined endless scrolling, this work looks at the labor and energy we give to our devices. It frames media consumption as a form of ritual.

The central idea is nourishment. Instead of feeding myself, I offer rice, water, coins, and torn notebook pages to an iPad, treating it as something that needs to be sustained. The actions are slow and deliberate, creating a sense of devotion but also exhaustion. My body becomes secondary. I perform the actions, but the device receives everything.

Each element carries meaning. Coins taken from a piggy bank represent value being spent rather than saved. Torn notebook pages reflect creativity being consumed. Occasional POV shots from the iPad suggest that the device is not passive, but watching and shaping us. Ambient sounds like coins, tearing paper, and pouring water reinforce the ritual and add to its tension.

The final overhead shot shows the imbalance. The space in front of the device is filled, while my own plate remains untouched. Nothing is left for myself.

This piece reflects how digital consumption is not just passive. It takes time, energy, and attention, often leaving very little behind.
