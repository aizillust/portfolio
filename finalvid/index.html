The Attention Field

Experimental Multi-Channel Video Project

Watch on YouTube

The Attention Field presents a multi-channel video that places the viewer inside a system constructing its own version of reality. It begins with error messages and a simulated reboot, establishing the perspective of a machine restarting and preparing to process information.

As the system loads, a desktop interface appears and begins selecting and arranging images. Everyday actions, such as a water pitcher opening or a lamp turning on, are presented as approved and controlled pieces of information. These moments feel stable, but they are already being filtered.

As more channels and layers are introduced, the feed becomes unstable. The system pulls in fragments from earlier projects and rearranges them into new contexts. Clips begin to slide, overlap, and glitch across the screen, losing their original meaning.

This buildup leads to a breakdown. The system can no longer maintain a coherent structure, and the image collapses into instability.

This piece reflects how digital systems do not simply show reality, but actively shape, filter, and reconstruct it.

Filmed on Phone + Tripod
Edited on Premiere Pro, Audition & After Effects
